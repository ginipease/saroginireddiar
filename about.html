Sarogini Reddiar is a visual artist whose work reimagines outdoor play and childlore, linking them with folkloric and cultural archetypes. Their art is an exploration of themes such as identity dispersion and the social adultification of black and brown children and their exclusion from play and outdoor education opportunities.  Their work is inspired by early years pedagogy, generational trauma theory, nature-based play, and societal fears around our interactions with the natural world. Using oils on canvas and acrylic ink on paper, cardboard, or board, pieces feature creatures and symbols, emblematic of the passing down of inter-generational stories of colonial childhood.
